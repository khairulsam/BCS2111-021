Welcome to Tunisia

Tunisia is a country located in North Africa. It has a rich history that includes Phoenician, Roman, and Arab influences, as well as a period of French colonialism. The country has a diverse culture that is influenced by its history and geography, with music, cuisine, and arts being important aspects. Tunisia is known for its beautiful beaches, ancient ruins, and stunning desert landscapes. The country has a population of approximately 12 million people and is governed by a presidential system. Its economy is based on a mix of agriculture, manufacturing, and meals, with tourism being a major contributor to the economy.

About Tunisia

Tunisia is a country in North Africa that offers a unique blend of history, culture, natural beauty, and warm hospitality. With a population of around 11.8 million people, Tunisia has a rich history that has been shaped by the Berber, Phoenician, Roman, and Arab civilizations. Visitors to Tunisia can explore ancient ruins such as the UNESCO World Heritage Site of Carthage, the Bardo Museum in Tunis, and the Medina of Tunis. The country's diverse landscapes include the Sahara Desert, the Atlas Mountains, and a stunning coastline along the Mediterranean Sea, offering visitors ample opportunities for outdoor activities such as hiking, swimming, and exploring.

Tunisia is also known for its delicious cuisine, which is a mix of Mediterranean and North African flavors. Some popular dishes include couscous, brik (a fried pastry filled with egg and tuna), and merguez (spicy sausages). Visitors can enjoy the country's cuisine at local restaurants or by visiting one of Tunisia's many outdoor markets.

Best Places

El Djem Amphitheater

 3rd century

The walls of the mighty Roman amphitheater of El Djem dwarf the surrounding modern town. This incredibly well-preserved Roman relic is Tunisia's big sightseeing highlight, one of the most popular things to do on day trips from the coastal resorts, and one of the best examples of amphitheater architecture left standing in the world.

Djerba

 9th or 8th century

If you're looking for the picture-perfect beach escape, then the island of Djerba checks all the right boxes. The island town of Houmt Souk is the main point of interest off the beach, with an old town district that is a muddle of whitewashed houses.

Carthage

 9th century

Once Rome's major rival, Carthage was the city of the seafaring Phoenicians forever memorialized in the Punic Wars. The atmospheric ruins of this ancient town now sit beside the sea amid the suburbs of Tunis, a warning that even the greatest cities can be reduced to rubble..

Read More

The National Bardo Museum

 19th-century

Even non-museum fans can't fail to be impressed at the massive haul of beautiful mosaics exhibited inside the Bardo. This is one of North Africa's top museums, and it houses one of the world's most important mosaic collections, all curated beautifully.

Read More

Sidi Bou Said

 13th-century

Impossibly cute, and amazingly photogenic, Sidi Bou Said is a clifftop village of petite dimensions that seem to have fallen off an artist's canvas. Unsurprisingly, artists have feted this little hamlet, now a high-class suburb of the capital Tunis, for decades.

Grand Erg Oriental

 -

Tunisia's vast Sahara covers much of the country's interior, and the most beautiful corner of the desert is the field of sand dunes known as the Grand Erg Oriental. These poetically beautiful dunes are a surreal and gorgeous landscape of huge waves, shaped by the ever-shifting desert sands.
